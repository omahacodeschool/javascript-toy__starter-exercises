In these exercises, we use prompt() to collect information, but most websites do that ask for information do not use prompt() -- how do they collect information (what HTML elements do they use)? What might be an advantage to doing it that way instead of with prompt()? Are there any disadvantages? Why might you use prompt()?

Most websites that collect information do so through HTML forms (or through click tracking, but that's a different kind of information). An advantage to HTML forms is that they can collect more than one piece of information at a time--a form with 10 text fields is the equivalent of 10 prompt pop-ups, which would get annoying for the user fast. Prompt would be acceptable if there's only one piece of information being collected--like a user entering his/her name at the beginning of a game.
