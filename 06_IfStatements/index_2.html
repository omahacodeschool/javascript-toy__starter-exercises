Does it matter if the user-provided password matches your password exactly, or is there some flexibility allowed in capitalization?

It needs to match exactly.

Did you use `=` or `==` or `===` to compare passwords (or something else)? Why? Would any of the others work?

I used '===' because out of the three that one is probably best suited to establishing equality, though '==' would also have worked. '=' would just have reset the variable to something else.

Why is it a bad idea to store a password (or other sensitive information) in plain text in a Javascript file?

Though I imagine that "duh" would probably suffice as an answer for this, generally speaking, you don't want to put something sensitive like a password in a place that's accessible to people who shouldn't have it. Putting it in plaintext does just that.
